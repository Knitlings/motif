Create

Draw a single repeat of your pattern—the preview will show how it tiles.

Paint

Click a cell to paint it with the active colour. Click and drag to paint multiple cells at once.

Erase

Click a painted cell again to toggle between the background colour and the active colour. Hold Shift and click cells to paint with the background colour instead of the active colour. Click the × below the grid to clear the entire grid to the background colour.

Resize

Drag the edges of the grid to change the grid dimensions, or use the grid settings panel.

Preview

The preview shows your pattern repeating, so you can see how your patterns connects across repeats and catch problems that only appear when tiled.

Colours

Click the colour panel button (top left) to open colour settings.

Adding pattern colours

Click the + button to add a new colour. You can add up to 20 colours per pattern.

Changing colours

Click any colour chip to open a colour picker and adjust it.

Removing colours

Hover over a colour chip and click the × to delete it. You'll be warned if cells use that colour.

Merging colours

Drag one colour chip onto another to merge them. The target colour becomes the dragged colour, and the dragged chip is removed.

Background colour

This is the default colour for empty cells. Hold Shift and click cells to paint with the background colour instead of the active colour.

Quick palette

Click any colour in the quick palette to set it as the active pattern colour. Hold Shift and click a colour in the quick palette to set it as the background colour.

Grid settings

Click the grid button (top right) to open grid settings.

Grid dimensions

Adjust how many columns and rows are displayed in your pattern grid. You can also change the grid size by dragging the edges of the grid.

Cell aspect ratio

Choose from preset ratios or set a custom ratio. This is useful if your craft requires non-square cells (like knitting, where stitches are wider than tall).

Undo and Redo

The curved arrow buttons below the canvas let you undo and redo your changes. Motif remembers your entire editing history for the current session.

Download

Your work is automatically saved as you go. Click the "Download pattern" dropdown below the grid to export your current pattern as an image file.

PNG

Download your pattern as a raster image.

SVG

Download your pattern as a vector graphic.

Import/Export

Click "Import/Export" in the top bar to create or load a previously saved JSON file. The JSON file contains your grid dimensions, cell aspect ratio, colour palette, pattern data, and preview settings in a human-readable format that can be manipulated programmatically.
